Estes Park is perhaps best known as a gateway to Rocky Mountain National
Park. But it's more than just a reprieve from the long drive on a day trip. 

Jaw-dropping scenery. Crisp mountain air. A charming downtown lined with
welcoming mom-and-pop shops and locally owned restaurants. Year-round
celebrations commemorating holidays, resident elk and a “Frozen Dead Guy” (more
on that later). Incredible outdoor activities that range from chill to thrill. 

Read on for a seasonal guide to visiting Estes Park.
