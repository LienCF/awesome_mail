 Boost your account security with these password best practices.

Smart password management can be crucial to keeping your accounts and personal information safe from hackers. This Cybersecurity Awareness Month, take charge of your online security with these password do’s and don’ts.
Do:



  
  


 -

Focus on length, not complexity, by creating a passphrase that is at least 15 characters long. They’re easier to remember and harder for hackers to crack.


  


 -

Create a unique passphrase that is only used for your Schwab accounts.


  


 -

 -

Consider using a trusted password manager to strengthen your online security. 

Don't:



  
  


 -

Share your passphrase or verification codes. When in doubt, only use a trusted phone number when calling a company.


  


 -

Use personal details, like your name or birthdate, in a passphrase.


  


 -

Ignore suspicious activity. Change your passphrase the moment you suspect that your account or login credentials have been compromised.


  


 -

Provide your login ID or passphrase on unverified websites, mobile applications, or text.
